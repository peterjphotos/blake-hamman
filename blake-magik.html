I grew up in a small rural Texas town with almost no access to the arts. A third-grade field trip to a high school production of Aesop's Falables changed everything — the moment I saw an actor leap from a jack-in-the-box, I knew I wanted to be on that stage. That one afternoon set the course of my life.

Why Children's Theatre

A church group gave me my first chance — fourth grade, The Best Christmas Pageant Ever, playing Ollie Herdman. The next year I was Wilbur in Charlotte's Web. When that program ended, so did my access to theatre — until high school, where I found it again performing Ugly Uncle Rumple in The Liberated Cinderella for 2nd through 5th graders from neighboring schools who crossed the street to see us. I did that children's show every year for four years.

Those experiences — as an audience member, then as a performer — are exactly why I want to direct children's theatre now. Someone gave me that gift. I want to spend my career giving it back.

On Magik Theatre: Magik is doing exactly what that church group did for me — putting professional-quality stories in front of children who may have no other access to live theatre. I know what it means to be that kid in the audience. I know what it means to be that kid on the stage. I want to be the director who makes that possible for the next generation — and I can't think of a better place to do it than here, in San Antonio, the city where I've built my career.
